  • About

    I am a third year PhD candidate at Visual Analytics and Imaging (VAI) Lab, Computer Science Department, Stony Brook University (Stony Brook, State University of New York), USA. My advisor is Prof. Klaus Mueller. In addition, I am also a research assistant at Computational Science Initiative, Brookhaven National Lab, USA. From Nov. 2015 to Feb. 2016, I held a scientific researcher position at Institute for Medical Informatics, Statistics, and Epidemiology (IMISE), Leipzig University, Germany, under the supervision of Dr. Peter Ahnert. From Jun.2015 to Sep.2015, I was a guest student and research assistant in the Department of Informatics, Friedrich Schiller University Jena, Germany, under the instruction of Prof. Joachim Giesen. From Mar. 2013 to Apr. 2015, I worked as a research assistant in the State University of New York, Korea campus with Prof. Klaus Mueller. I obtained my B.Eg degree in Software Engineering from Shandong University, China and an MS degree in Computer Science from Stony Brook University, USA at 2013 and 2016 respectively.

    I fall love with data. My primary research interest includes visual analytics, information visualization and scientific visualization with a special focus on the High-Dimensional (Multidimensional/Multivariate) data. Specifically, I work on high dimensional data layouts (embeddings), time-series data visual analytics, geospatial data visual analytics, social and supercomputing network/graph visual analytics, bioinformatics etc. I am a student member of IEEE, and a reviewer of IEEE Transactions on Visualization and Computer Graphics and IEEE Visualization conference 2015 and 2016. I obtained the IT Consilience Creative Program Scholarship from South Korea from 2014-2016.
